# 🌾 Business Insights

## Project Summary

A comprehensive analysis was performed on **40,480 sugarcane transactions** from Season 14 to understand production patterns, operational efficiency, and geographical distribution.

---

# Key Business Insights

## 1. Overall Production

- Total Production: **444,241.98 Tons**
- Average Net Weight: **10.97 Tons**
- Total Transactions: **40,480**

The season processed a significant volume of sugarcane with consistent daily operations.

---

## 2. Shift Performance

Shift **C** delivered the highest overall production, followed by Shift **B** and Shift **A**.

This indicates that Shift C contributed the largest share of seasonal production.

---

## 3. Harvesters

A small number of harvesters contributed a large percentage of the total production.

This suggests that harvester performance has a significant impact on factory throughput.

---

## 4. Cultivators

Production was concentrated among a limited number of cultivators.

The factory depends heavily on high-performing growers.

---

## 5. Plantation Area

Most plantations ranged between **2 and 5 acres**.

Plantation areas around **3–5 acres** contributed the highest overall production.

Extremely large plantation area values were identified as data outliers.

---

## 6. Zone Analysis

Factory Zone supplied the highest total production.

Some external zones processed fewer trips but achieved higher average production per trip.

---

## 7. Taluka Analysis

Production varied significantly across talukas.

Certain talukas consistently delivered higher production than others.

---

## 8. Village Analysis

A few villages contributed a substantial portion of the total seasonal production.

These villages represent major supply locations.

---

## 9. Distance Analysis

Most sugarcane was transported within the **11–30 km** distance range.

Long-distance transportation generally involved larger loads per trip.

---

## 10. Cane Quality

Production was concentrated in a limited number of cane quality categories.

Monitoring cane quality can help improve overall factory efficiency.

---

## 11. Correlation Analysis

Net Weight showed meaningful relationships with several operational variables.

Correlation analysis identified the most influential factors affecting production.

---

## 12. Outlier Detection

A small number of unusually high production records were identified.

These records should be validated before being used for future forecasting models.

---

# Conclusion

The analysis indicates that sugarcane production is primarily influenced by:

- Geographic location
- Plantation size
- Harvesting operations
- Transportation patterns
- Operational shifts

These insights provide valuable information for improving harvesting efficiency, transport planning, and production management.
